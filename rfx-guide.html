Introduction to RFx

RFx is an umbrella term used for Request for Information (RFI), Request for Proposal (RFP), and Request for Quote (RFQ). Each phase plays a critical role in procurement processes and vendor management.

Comparison of RFI, RFP, and RFQ

Aspect RFI (Request for Information) RFP (Request for Proposal) RFQ (Request for Quote)
Think of it as Tell me what's possible Show me your best solution Tell me your price for this specific need
What Client Wants
  • Information about available solutions
  • Market research
  • Understanding capabilities
  • No immediate buying decision
  • Complete solution to their problem
  • Innovative approaches
  • Best value for money
  • Detailed implementation plan
  • Exact pricing for defined requirements
  • Quick turnaround
  • Meets specified requirements
  • Best price for standard solution
How to Respond
  • Share your company's capabilities
  • Provide high-level information
  • Include relevant experience
  • Keep it informative, not sales-focused
  • No detailed pricing needed
  • Focus on best technical solution
  • Show understanding of requirements
  • Demonstrate unique value
  • Include detailed methodology
  • Competitive pricing with justification
  • Focus on meeting exact specifications
  • Provide most competitive pricing
  • Keep it simple and direct
  • Show compliance to requirements
